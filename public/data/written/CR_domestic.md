Costa Rica is [undergoing a phased reopening](https://www.garda.com/crisis24/news-alerts/364821/costa-rica-authorities-announce-economic-reopening-from-august-1-update-11). Regulations vary across the country — each canton has been designated with an alert status that is either orange or yellow.

- From August 1, 2020, cantons under the **orange alert status** will undergo alternating phases, reopening for nine days, followed by 12 days of closure. As such, these cantons have the following schedule in place:
  - August 1, 2020 – August 9, 2020: Businesses and restaurants are permitted to reopen.
  - August 10, 2020 – August 21, 2020: The closure phase will come into effect.
  - August 22, 2020 – August 30, 2020: Businesses and restaurants are permitted to reopen.
  - [Vehicle restrictions]([https://sites.google.com/presidencia.go.cr/alertas/restricci%C3%B3n-vehicular](https://sites.google.com/presidencia.go.cr/alertas/restricción-vehicular)) based on license plate numbers will continue to apply throughout the orange alert.
- Cantons under the **yellow alert status** may reopen and resume operations throughout August.

These measures may change without warning according to the epidemiological situation. [Click here](https://sites.google.com/presidencia.go.cr/alertas/alerta-por-cant%C3%B3n) for full details on the designated orange and yellow alert cantons.

The following measures continue to apply nationwide:

- Large gatherings are prohibited.
- Face masks must be worn in most indoor public areas.
- Certain entertainment venues such as bars and nightclubs remain closed.
- Public transportation operations have resumed, subject to capacity limits.
